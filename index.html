Hello, I am Jang Yena, a graphic designer from Korea.
When you connect the dots of a person's past and present, you can see the line
that connects their past passions and how they have led to the present.
Also, by extending this line of past and present into the future, I would like to share
what kind of future you can expect when working with me.

Who you were?

At the age of 16,I was a bit different from those around me. During my school years, I saw textbooks not as a means to perform well on exams but as a gateway to discovering new thoughts in the world. I started with textbooks, adding annotations and creating mind maps, often moved to tears by something that fascinated me secretly during class (Researching mind maps alone during middle and high school - 698 sheets). Teachers suggested that I would have been better off born in France. This planted a dream of Europe, representing the unknown to me, and a deep desire to explore the unvisited France.

How did you end up in Europe?

At the age of 21, after going through academic pursuits that did not align with me, I became an English instructor at an academy teaching middle and high school students. I believed that studying English would help me solidify the dream of Europe that had been vague since my childhood. However, being curious beyond the given textbooks, the role of an English teacher felt dull to me, so I embarked on my first solo trip to Europe.

Although I didn't know much about Europe, I first arrived in the 'France' that I often heard about in my childhood. I visited art boulevards in France and struck up conversations with people there. While staying at the house of an art student named Juliet in the city of Lyon, I decided to study art in Europe after talking all night about art and Europe. Although I didn't have the opportunity to visit the Netherlands directly, Juliet, through friends, was well-informed about Dutch design, art, and life. Intrigued by the experimental and artistic design of the Netherlands and its international and English-friendly environment, I applied to the Graphic Design program at KABK after returning from my trip to France.

What do I pursue meaning?

At the age of 26, every year-end, I take some time to reflect on the year by quietly enjoying a half-body bath. In the midst of busy days, it is challenging to find time for in-depth contemplation of long-term goals or values. However, during the year-end, I take a few days to write down the ideas in my mind on a large A0-sized canvas, organizing my thoughts. Clear values enable me to make decisions that are true to myself in various situations, so I try to summarize them as a motto for the new year and write them on the window to remind myself. What am I interested in?

At the age of 23, I had found the direction of design I should pursue in my very personal life. I had a keen interest in "Memory." Knowing my own identity, I realized that stories only about me, as commercial experiences, were not what I wanted. My memory is a narrative, incomplete but full. The memories I keep in my heart, constantly changing and evolving, are what I want to express through design. I want to create works that are not just visually appealing but also meaningful, resonating with the memories and experiences of others.

What design do I pursue?

Personally, I do not believe that design will save the world. While there are occasional moments where design can solve the problems existing in the world, the role of a designer is to explore and express the beauty and meaning of life. I want to create designs that are not just functional but also evoke emotions and tell stories. For the past few years, I have been exploring various fields of design, from graphic design to product design. I want to be a designer who not only creates beautiful and functional works but also makes people think and feel. My goal is to create designs that are not just visually appealing but also meaningful and thought-provoking.

Graduation plans?

Whatever I want to achieve, I want to enjoy it. When I am faced with a question like the one I faced at the age of 17, I want to be able to answer it with confidence. I believe that design is not just about creating visually appealing works but also about understanding the world and its various forms, trying to find meaning in it. I plan to continue this journey of exploration and reflection in the years to come. After graduation, I want to work in a field where I can apply my knowledge and skills as a designer.
